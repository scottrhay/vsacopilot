# VSA Prompt Packet — Microsoft 365 Copilot

Copy, paste, replace the [brackets]. Every prompt on this sheet works in your environment today.
The one rule that never fails: **attach the file.** July 2026.

## Session A — Prompting & Copilot Chat

**The framework: G C S E** — Goal (the deliverable) · Context (who it's for) · Source (attach the file) · Expectations (format, length, tone).

1. **Absorb a long document**
   `Summarize the attached [policy/report] for [audience]: 5 bullets on what changed, plain language, include section numbers.`
2. **Brief leadership**
   `Using the attached report, draft a one-page brief for [leader]: progress summary, top 3 risks with owners, decisions needed this week.`
3. **Tighten anything**
   `Remove any sentence without a decision, a number, or an action.`
4. **Iterate — the four verbs** (follow-ups, no re-attaching needed)
   `Rewrite this for [different audience].` · `Reorganize as: summary, findings, risks, next steps.` · `Keep only the top 3 items by impact.` · `Cut it to half the length, keep all numbers.`
5. **Power move: let it interview you**
   `Interview me one question at a time to build the best prompt for [task].`
6. **Power move: self-critique**
   `Critique your answer and rewrite it better.`

7. **Borrow an expert voice**
   `Act as a [range safety officer / contracts specialist / communications director]. Review the attached document and flag what your role would catch.`
8. **The skeptical executive**
   `You are a skeptical executive reviewer. What three questions would you ask before approving this?`

*Tip: the Prompt Gallery (Create, in the Copilot app) has proven prompts by task and role — save your winners and share the team's.*

### Validate anything
9. **Make it show its work**
   `List your source for each claim in that answer - one line per claim.`
10. **Pin the claim to the page**
   `Quote the exact sentence from the attached document that supports [claim].`
11. **Surface its doubt**
   `What in this answer are you least confident about, and why?`

## Session B — Teams & Outlook

12. **Thread archaeology** (Outlook "Summary by Copilot", or paste the thread into Chat)
   `Summarize this thread: decisions made, who owes what, deadlines mentioned.`
13. **The reply pattern — confirm, commit, ask**
   `Draft a reply confirming [X], committing to [Y] by [date], and asking who owns [Z]. Professional, under 150 words.`
14. **Coach your risky email** (Outlook Coaching)
   `Review this draft for tone and clarity. My reader is [who]; I need them to [do what].`

15. **The commitments table**
   `Who owes what, by when, in this thread? Table: person, item, deadline.`
16. **Grounded reply with receipts**
   `Using the attached [agreement/policy], answer this question and cite the section: [question]`

### Personalize it — Copilot in YOUR voice
*Once, 15 minutes: copy ~10 sent emails you're proud of, then —*

17. **Capture your voice**
   `These are emails I wrote. Analyze my writing style and produce a reusable style guide: tone, sentence rhythm, structure habits, greetings and sign-offs. Then add a NEVER list: buzzwords and AI-sounding phrases that never appear in my writing (e.g. leverage, delve, synergy, "I hope this finds you well"). Be specific.`
18. **Draft as you**
   `Using this style guide: [paste profile] - draft [the email you need]. Keep all facts; the voice is non-negotiable and nothing from the never list.`

*Save the profile to a Page named "My voice" (find it in Library). Refresh quarterly. The never list is what keeps drafts sounding like you instead of AI - be ruthless with it.*
19. **Meeting prep brief**
    `Using the attached docs, build a one-page prep brief for my meeting with [who]: their obligations, our commitments, open items, 3 questions I should ask.`
20. **Timed agenda**
    `Turn this brief into a [45]-minute agenda with time boxes; put open items first.`
21. **Follow-up email**
    `Draft a follow-up email from these meeting notes: decisions, action items with owners, next meeting date. Brief and friendly.`

## Session C — PowerPoint & Word

22. **Grounded draft** (Word — reference your files)
    `Write a [brief/memo/summary] covering [topics] based on [reference files]. Include owners and dates.`
23. **Audience rewrite** (select text first)
    `Rewrite this selection for [audience]: [tone], half the length, keep all numbers.`
24. **Interrogate a document**
    `What does this document say about [topic]? Point me to the section.`
25. **Inherit a deck** (PowerPoint)
    `Summarize this presentation in 5 bullets for someone presenting it tomorrow.`
26. **Fix a wall-of-words slide** (select the text box)
    `Rewrite as max 6 words per bullet, keep all numbers.`
27. **Navigate a big deck**
    `Which slides mention [topic]?`

### Evaluate anything — rubric first
28. **Build the rubric (before reading options)**
   `From this specification, build a weighted evaluation rubric: 5-6 criteria, weights summing to 100, one line on what excellent looks like.`
29. **Score with receipts**
   `Score each option against this rubric. Table: criterion, weight, score, weighted total. Cite your source per score.`
30. **Capture the dissent**
   `Argue FOR the lowest scorer - steelman it in 5 bullets. Then draft the recommendation memo with a Dissent Considered section.`
31. **Contract obligations sweep**
   `List every obligation on OUR side of this agreement, with section numbers and deadlines.`

## Session D — OneNote & Excel

*Excel tip: format your data as a named table first (select data → Insert → Table). Copilot reasons far better over tables.*

32. **Formula from plain English**
    `Write a formula for [calculation] using the [TableName] table. Handle [edge case, e.g. divide by zero].`
33. **Decode an inherited formula**
    `Explain this formula like I'm not an Excel person: [paste formula]`
34. **Debug loop**
    `This returned [error]. Fix it.`
35. **What-if framing**
    `I want to model [scenario]. What columns and formulas should I add?`
36. **Notes into a safety net** (OneNote — or attach the notes in Chat)
    `Extract every action item from these notes as a table: action, owner, deadline. Flag anything with no owner.`
37. **Notes for the status call**
    `Summarize these notes in 5 bullets for a status call — decisions first.`
38. **Minutes you can send**
    `Rewrite these notes as professional meeting minutes I can send to [who].`

*Keep a Page named "My prompts" - every prompt that works goes in; share the winners with your team.*

## Your first week

Mon: summarize a thread (#12) · Tue: draft one section (#22) · Wed: ask one formula (#32) · Thu: refine one notes page (#36) · Fri: share a win with your team.

*Remember: Copilot drafts, you decide. Verify numbers and names against the source before anything leaves you.*

## Responsible AI — your daily checklist

**Review before you share** — every output is a first draft; check accuracy, tone, and fit. · **Protect sensitive data** — nothing classified, regulated, or personal in a prompt without authorization. · **Watch for bias** — scrutinize outputs about people, decisions, or recommendations. · **Verify the receipts** — click a citation before a number leaves you. · **Disclose when appropriate** — know your organization's policy on AI-assisted work.

## Which app for which job

| You need to... | Go to |
|---|---|
| Absorb, draft, or brief from documents | Copilot Chat (attach the file) |
| Triage threads, draft and coach replies | Outlook |
| Draft and rewrite documents, interrogate long files | Word |
| Summarize, tighten, and navigate decks | PowerPoint |
| Formulas, explanations, what-ifs | Excel |
| Turn notes into actions and minutes | OneNote |
| Keep a great answer | Send it to a Page (Library) |
