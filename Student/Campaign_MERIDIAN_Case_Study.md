# Campaign MERIDIAN — the story behind your training

**A learner-facing case study for the Virginia Spaceport Authority Copilot training series.**
*Everything here is invented for training. Skyward Dynamics, the Meridian LV-2 campaign, and every name, date, and figure are fictional. No real customer, mission, or contract is described.*

## Why this exists

Every demo in all four sessions — and every prompt in your packet — points at one shared story: a single launch campaign. You never have to learn a new scenario mid-hour, and by Session D the campaign feels like a program you actually work on. That's deliberate: the skills stick because the story doesn't change.

## The one-paragraph version

**Skyward Dynamics**, a commercial launch company, is the newest tenant at the spaceport — their **Meridian LV-2** campaign targets a launch window opening **July 28** from **Pad B**. Integration is 82% complete, the wet dress rehearsal just moved onto the same dates as Pad B's annual deluge inspection, and the **Launch Readiness Review is July 24**. The whole campaign is watching one question: **does the window hold — and who owes what, by when, to get there?**

## The threads you'll pull

1. **The schedule collision.** The wet dress rehearsal now lands on the Pad B deluge-system inspection window (Jul 20–22). Compress the inspection for $4,200, slip the rehearsal to the 22nd and burn the contingency day, or move a fire-marshal visit that's stacked on the same day. Somebody has to decide by Thursday — that decision is buried in a 12-message email thread.

2. **The paperwork blockers.** Two items gate the campaign and nobody owns them yet: the tenant's **$100M insurance certificate** (required 45 days before first launch by their use agreement — it's still not on file, and it appears in the risk register as R-121 with owner "UNASSIGNED") and four pad crew whose **Category-3 range safety certifications** lapse before the window (the new Rev 7 policy tightened recertification to 12 months).

3. **The new rulebook.** Range Safety Policy **Revision 7** just landed: visitor escorts tightened to 1:1, the lightning standoff for fueling doubled to 10 nautical miles, and launch-day staffing certs got stricter. Forty-eight pages — and your leadership wants to know what changed by lunch.

## How to use this

Skim it once before your first session — five minutes. Then in class, when the instructor says "the campaign," you'll know exactly which campaign, whose insurance cert is missing, and why July 24 matters. After the series, these files are yours to practice on: every prompt in the VSA Prompt Packet works against them.
